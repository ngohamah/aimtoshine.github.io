Story

Aim To Shine was built to mentor the leaders of tomorrow who are from a vulnerable background with a lack of opportunities and resources to build a brighter and better future. The Initiative focuses on primary students between the ages of 6 to 15 years, from grade 1 to grade 7. In 2018 the founder was inspired by how students were coming to her for their homework assistance, and she started to gather them every day to teach them basic Math and English. After two months, she had more than twenty students coming for help. The number grew significantly every day, and she realised the need for others who could help provide the service. She approached her friend for assistance. Since then, she has been motivated by the impact the project has had on the lives of these children. In 2019 when she began her university education, she shared her idea about the initiative with one of her lecturers interested in social impact. Her lecturer helped her in bring her plans for the Initiative to life. With the help of nine mentors, Aim To Shine is able to provide valuable support services to the vulnerable community of Chitungwiza.

What We Do

What We Do

Teaching

We teach students Mathematics and the English language and help them understand various topics of their coursework they may be struggling with.

Mentorship

Our mentors offer socioemotional support to students as well as tailored guidance as a way of helping them cope with stress and societal expectations.

Scholarship

We offer learning materials to students as well as finanical support towards their effective learning and development.

Training

We train students in basic hygiene, reproductive health, consenting, career development and entrepreneurship.

Design Thinking Projects

We encourage students to adopt a user-centered approach when developing solutions to problems

Programming (Information Systems)

We teach students basic concepts of programming such as programming in HTML and CSS
